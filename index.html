N/L/A/W

Newton Local Area Wargamers

N/L/A/W came about from a recognition that getting together around a table to play games can have a huge range of social benefits. It was first founded in 2022 as part of Ascent Collages efforts to expand its student's education beyond just the basic curriculum, however after changes made to how DofE clubs could operate and adjustments to the college's operating times caused us to have to stop hosting on-campus meetings we decided to form a dedicated hobby club since its members felt they were benefited greatly from participating. The club felt that they all benefit in many ways from getting together weekly and wanted to not only continue this but increase their interactions and share those benefits with the wider community.

We're a not-for-profit organisation which is run and staffed by volunteers who historically have helped run after-hours clubs at the college where the club started. The club has a relaxed atmosphere, with a mix of people from 16 to 60 who enjoy playing a variety of tabletop games, ranging from wargames; such as Warhammer 40,000, RPGs such as Dungeons and Dragons and more classic board games. Though at our heart, we're more focused on RPGs and tabletop wargaming. We aim to provide the local community of game enthusiasts to meet and collectively enjoy their hobby together. We're always welcome to curious members of the community coming along and hopefully even returning to visit in the future.

Tabletop gaming and roleplaying games have many health benefits for those who participate. Ranging from social interactions when playing games or engaging with other hobbyists, mindfulness benefits when working on the more creative hobby aspects, such as painting minis or crafting scenery. Simple engineering concepts when constructing minis or scratching building scenery. Helping stretch your creative legs via painting minis or writing stories and background for them. The games themselves can help with basic maths skills, due to the need to add up in game costs of armies to ensure a rough balance of armies on the tabletop, to calculate the outcomes of dice rolls and gauging percentage chances when playing.
